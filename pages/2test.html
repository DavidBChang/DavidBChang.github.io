Payload

As the payload manager of my NASAâ€™s Student Launch team, I learned to draft Arduino schematics and algorithm, which I used to program a CO2 sensor, our payload. The CO2 sensor would report the levels of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere during both the ascent and descent of the rocket. The components of the control board include the Teensy, Lipo battery, Pnut altimeter, K30 CO2 sensor, and the Micro SD card. I timed the CO2 sensor to start recording data onto the SD card by sensing an increase in altitude of the rocket, which signifies launch, and it would stop recording data onto the SD card when the altitude does not fluctuate after five checks, signifying a land. Because of my software and hardware experience with TARC, it was easier for me to design and test my project.
